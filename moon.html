THE MOON

The Lunar Ambiance

OVERVIEW

The moon is Earth’s one and only natural satellite which remains as the fifth largest moon within the solar system. Its surface was a result upon a giant-impact of Earth and a small planet, allowing for a collision where its debris are attracted to the Earth’s gravitational force, resulting in a sustained orbit. The moon itself causes many events which affect Earth, including predictable rises and falls of the sea level and causing Earth to experience a slight deceleration in its rotational speed, where days tend to increase by 2.3 milliseconds per century. Constructing a human colony on the moon might just be one of the greatest achievements and beginnings of human space colonization, as by extracting valuable resources from its surfaces, mankind are able to easily construct revolutionary rockets for multiplanetary missions.

NASA MOON MISSION

ARTEMIS PROGRAM

NASA’s Artemis Program aims to launch manned missions to the moon in hopes of terraforming its environmental conditions such that minor human colonies could be established. This mission highlights the importance of the moon as a natural satellite to the ambitious goals of human multiplanetary colonization, as establishing a colony requires a considerable amount of energy and resources.
