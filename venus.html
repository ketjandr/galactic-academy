VENUS

Morning and Evening Star

OVERVIEW

Venus is the second planet closest to the sun within the solar system and remains as the closest planet to Earth. It is the hottest planet in the solar system in terms of surface temperature, able to reach 460℃ at times. Venus and Earth possess some geophysical similarities, including size, atmospheric pressure, mass, and elemental composition. The planet’s atmosphere is highly hazy and thick, making it quite difficult to analyze its volcanic and blazing surface. Its atmospheric elemental abundance of carbon dioxide is caused by the absence of water and plants to absorb the toxic gases. However, Venus is potentially viable for human colonization as despite its scorching temperatures and toxic atmosphere, its geophysical properties are similar to Earth’s, where cloud cities are able to be constructed to begin the phase of terraformation.

NASA VENUS MISSION

MARINER-10

The first extraterrestrial mission to Venus was conducted on November 3, 1973, where NASA’s Mariner-10 artificial satellite was launched to obtain photographs of Mercury and Venus. Mariner-10 predominantly studied Venus’ gravitational force and atmospheric conditions. The primary achievement of the satellite was when it managed to discover the toxic atmosphere within Venus which made the planet look quite different when viewed from a telescope.
