PHASE 1: RESEARCH AND INVESTIGATION

Research and investigation involves immensely analysing a potential colonized planet, where space vehicles such as artificial satellites and mechanical rovers are deployed within the vicinity of the planet. These vehicles implement scientific instruments to gather crucial data about the planet’s temperature, elemental composition, atmospheric pressure, gravitational acceleration, etc. Once the required information about the planet has been obtained, scientists are able to install various strategies to construct a habitable environment and ultimately prepare for extraterrestrial manned missions.

PHASE 2: ENVIRONMENTAL ADJUSTMENTS

Crucial information about the planets would allow significant adjustments done to the planet’s environment such that its surrounding will become more Earth-like. This includes adjusting the temperature either by cooling it with comets or heating it with the greenhouse effect, creating oxygen by nurturing soils and planting trees, and generating water by melting polar ice caps. A controlled environment although would inevitably consume a lot of resources and time to successfully achieve, will ease the possibility of increased manned missions to the planet to terraform the planet from its inside.

PHASE 3: MANNED MISSIONS

Conducting manned missions to the environmental territory of the planet once its factors have been controlled will further improve the progress towards a true human colony. These manned missions will deploy experienced astronauts which are competently trained to survive within the planet given its unorthodox conditions. With the deployment of humans, the process of environmental adjustments could be fully included whilst also constructing spaces of living such as domes and life-bubbles. Most importantly, by involving the direct descent of human astronauts into the vicinity, experimental procedures to prepare for an authentic human colony could be easily administered.

PHASE 4: HUMAN COLONIZATION

Once the planet’s environment is suitable for life and spaces of living has been constructed, domestic flights towards these extraterrestrial destinations would fully complete the process of terraformation. Humans are able to populate and reproduce within the vicinity of the planet as well as implement a governmental system to organize the apprehended societies. Services such as healthcare and education would be widely available, resulting in an advanced civilization outside the Earth. Human colonization would ultimately mark the successful efforts of planet terraformation.

REVOLUTIONARY MISSION

DYSON SPHERE

Discover how harnessing the energy exerted by the sun is theoretically possible by humanity’s efforts of constructing a Dyson Sphere. This colossal megastructure encompasses the sun to capture most of its energy, consisting of solar collectors which directly absorb the potential energy from the sun’s rays. An advanced civilization would highly benefit from the energy output of a star, where a star the size of the sun would produce enough energy for the human civilization to last for 1 million years within 1 second.

UPCOMING MISSION

PERSEVERANCE ROVER

The Perseverance rover is a prominent component of NASA’s Mars Mission, as it is constructed to be deployed on Mars by 2020. Perseverance was launched with the Atlas V-541 rocket from Launch Site Complex 41 at Cape Canaveral, Florida. It is deployed within the Martian soil to mainly seek signs of ancient life and collect samples of the Martian rock and soil to be brought back to Earth. The rover will reside upon the Red Planet for roughly 687 Earth days, which equates to approximately 1 Mars year.

GOVERNMENT PROGRAM

ARTEMIS PROGRAM

NASA’s Artemis Program aims to land the first woman and the next man into the moon, following the previously conducted Apollo Program. This program is constructed to implement modern aerospace technology to explore and investigate the lunar surface in hopes of using it as a space base for future manned Mars missions. NASA is implementing the Space Launch System rocket, one of the most powerful reusable rockets known to mankind.

TECHNOLOGICAL PHILOSOPHY

KARDASHEV SCALE

Explore how advanced civilizations are arranged with the Kardashev Scale, a technological philosophy which measures a civilization’s technological development based on power consumption, energy production, and innovative extensions. As proposed by Soviet cosmonaut Nikolai Kardashev in 1964, the scale itself possesses 7 distinct stages of civilization advancements, where each stage possesses a power consumption which exponentially rises as the scale moves on.
