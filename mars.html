MARS

The Red Planet

OVERVIEW

Mars is the fourth planet closest to the sun and is the terrestrial planet closest to the asteroid belt intersecting the orbit of Jupiter. Mars possesses a rocky and cold surface below its thin yet hazy atmosphere. It is famously remarked as the Red Planet since its surface material consists of a high concentration of iron oxide, producing a blood red hue due to the chemical oxidation with the planet’s atmospheric elements. Mars possesses a rich amount of polar ice caps and clouds in the atmosphere, signifying a possibility for the presence of water and therefore life. However, its thin and toxic atmosphere prone to solar wind radiations cause it nearly impossible for life to settle within the harsh conditions of the planet. Humans are highly interested in terraforming Mars as it is one of the closest planets to Earth and the plausible presence of liquid water.

SPACE-X MARS MISSION

RED DRAGON PROGRAM

Space-X’s mission to Mars, known as the Red Dragon Program, was indicated to test techniques and technology to conduct manned missions to the planet itself. This program aims to deploy space vehicles such as rovers and artificial satellites such that a pathway for future manned missions are able to be conducted with maximum efficiency. However, the development for the Red Dragon was halted and the space company moved on with the execution of Starlink missions.
